Homework 7: Part 1 ~ Styles

When I was younger, my weekends were always spent wandering around the flea market with my cousins. We’d walk through the crowded lanes, stopping at every table with old books, mismatched dishes, or strange objects that looked like they had lived a thousand lives before ending up there.

One time, we found a tiny wooden chest carved with stars and vines. The man selling it didn’t know where it came from, but he told us his grandmother used to hide small treasures inside it. We bought it with the few dollars we had and spent the rest of the day imagining what secrets it might have held.

Even now, whenever I come across something second-hand, I think about that old chest and the stories we invented for it. It made me realize that sometimes the best things aren’t shiny or new. They’re the things with a little mystery, a little wear, and a lot of character.
